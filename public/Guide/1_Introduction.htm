Introduction

Overview

RidePilot is an open source, web-based Computer Aided Scheduling and Dispatch (CASD) software system designed to meet the needs of small scale human service Demand-Response Transportation (DRT) agencies. Originally developed for Ride Connection in Portland, OR, RidePilot allows authorized users to:

·         Maintain Customer, Driver, and Vehicle information

·         Manually create and maintain Trips and Runs

·         Allow third-party application (e.g., 1-Click|CS) to request Trips

·         Assign Trip to Runs

·         Generate Reports from virtually any tables within the underlying database

User Types

RidePilot is designed to serve the needs of many different types of users, with features and functions appropriate for each one:

·         Customers are individuals who will be assisted by RidePilot Users to meet their DRT service needs. While they cannot log directly into RidePilot, accounts must be created for each one.

·         Users directly use RidePilot when assisting Customers, creating and maintaining Trips and Runs, and assigning Trips to Runs.

·         Editors are advanced Users who can access additional functionality within RidePilot.

·         Provider Administrators are representatives of organizations that provide DRT services. They can manage and maintain information on the Provider.

·         System Administrators are the “super-users” who take care of the RidePilot software.

The functionality available to each of these user types are described in this Guide.
